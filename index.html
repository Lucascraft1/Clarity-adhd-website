Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Do you take insurance?

Clarity ADHD is a cash-pay practice and does not bill insurance directly. However, we provide superbills — itemized receipts with diagnosis and procedure codes — that you can submit to your insurance for potential out-of-network reimbursement. HSA and FSA cards are accepted.

Who can be seen at Clarity ADHD?

Adults 18 and older who are physically located in Washington State at the time of their appointment. All visits are conducted via secure video — no in-person appointments are offered.

Can stimulant medications be prescribed via telehealth?

Yes. Under current federal and Washington State regulations, controlled substances — including stimulant medications — can be prescribed via telehealth following a thorough evaluation. Clarity ADHD complies with all DEA and state requirements, including prescription monitoring and electronic prescribing.

What if I'm already on ADHD medication?

Many patients come to Clarity ADHD already on medication that isn't working as well as it could. Your evaluation will include a full review of your current and past medications, and your treatment plan will build from what we learn — whether that means adjusting, switching, or optimizing what you're already taking.

What is a PA-C?

A Physician Assistant–Certified (PA-C) is a licensed medical provider who evaluates, diagnoses, and prescribes medication. Lucas Craft, MMS, PA-C practices in collaboration with a licensed physician in accordance with Washington State law (RCW 18.71A), with a clinical focus on adult ADHD and commonly co-occurring conditions.

Do you provide therapy or crisis services?

No. Clarity ADHD provides psychiatric evaluation and medication management only. If you are experiencing a mental health emergency, call or text 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline) or call 911. For therapy, we're happy to coordinate care with your existing therapist or provide referrals.
